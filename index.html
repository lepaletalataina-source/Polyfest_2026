Polyfest 2026!!

Polyfest 2026 will be held across two venues for the first time, reflecting its incredible growth and continued importance. From March 18–21 at Manukau Sports Bowl and March 30–April 2 at the Due Drop Events Centre, thousands of students will come together to celebrate their cultures through dance, song, language, and identity.

Each stage—Māori, Samoan, Tongan, Niuean, Cook Islands, and more—will showcase the unique traditions of Aotearoa's diverse communities. With energetic performances, stunning costumes, and powerful cultural expression, the festival connects generations and strengthens cultural pride.

Polyfest is more than an event—it's a movement that celebrates identity, builds leadership, and preserves cultural heritage. Whether you're a student, supporter, or visitor, there's something unforgettable waiting for you. Don't miss out!!

Polyfest 2026 continues to be a vibrant celebration of Polynesian cultures, bringing together communities from all over New Zealand and beyond. It is a unique opportunity for young people to showcase their heritage and foster intercultural understanding.

Each performance stage offers a rich experience of traditional and contemporary dances, music, and storytelling. The festival not only honors ancient customs but also embraces modern expressions of identity and creativity.

Community involvement is at the heart of Polyfest. Families, schools, and local organizations collaborate to make the event a meaningful celebration of culture, history, and pride, inspiring future generations to carry these traditions forward.
